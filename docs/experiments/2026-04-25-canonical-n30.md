# 2026-04-25 — `full-5spec-canonical-n30`: do hand-engineered features contribute?

- **Status:** Complete
- **Started:** 2026-04-23 (after `n30-mle-vs-disable` finished)
- **Finished:** 2026-04-25 00:03 UTC
- **Wall-clock runtime:** ~33 h for 150 runs (12.1 min/run mean train_time)

## Goal

Test the foundational post-bakeoff question: **does ANY of the hand-
engineered missingness-features pipeline contribute to mechanism
classification at committee-grade power?** Headline paired comparison:
`all_disabled` (no missingness features) vs `disable_littles` (the
surviving baseline from `n30-mle-vs-disable`).

Either outcome is paper-worthy. "All_disabled ≈ surviving baseline"
contradicts a decade of tabular-missingness practice and would be the
dissertation's headline methodological finding. "All_disabled clearly
worse" gives the methods-section feature-group decomposition.

## Interpretation

### Headline finding: **the hand-engineered missingness-features pipeline contributes**

Pre-registered headline test: paired `all_disabled − disable_littles`
on accuracy. **CI [−0.095, −0.021] excludes zero in the negative
direction**, wilcoxon p=0.0066, perm p=0.0060, d_z=−0.56. Removing the
entire missingness-features path costs ~6 percentage points of
accuracy at committee-grade power.

This **rejects** the post-bakeoff hypothesis that the network can
learn equivalent signal from raw tokens + observation mask alone.
Classical missingness-pattern statistics are doing real work here.

### Sub-finding: it's NOT the MCAR slot doing the work

The MCAR slot (cached Little's MLE) is the one feature group that
*hurts*. The non-MCAR groups (`missing_rate_stats`, `cross_column_corr`)
are what contribute:

- The 6-pp lift between `all_disabled` and `disable_littles` is
  attributable to the joint contribution of missing_rate_stats +
  cross_column_corr (the only difference between those two specs).
- The MLE Little's slot, layered on top of those, *subtracts* ~3 pp
  (`baseline` vs `disable_littles`, replicating the
  `n30-mle-vs-disable` finding at the same power).
- Both `disable_missing_rate` and `disable_cross_column` are
  *worse than `all_disabled`* — having the MLE slot on without one of
  its companion non-MCAR groups is worse than having no missingness
  features at all. The MLE harm dominates the surviving non-MCAR
  group's individual benefit.

Consistent picture: classical MCAR-detection statistics (every family
the bakeoff screened) contribute zero or negative; non-MCAR
missingness-pattern statistics (rate variation, cross-column
correlation) contribute about +6 pp jointly.

### Limitation: individual decomposition not cleanly identified

The single-disable specs (`disable_missing_rate`, `disable_cross_column`)
each carry the known-harmful MLE slot, so we can't cleanly attribute
the +6-pp joint lift between the two non-MCAR groups. To isolate
their individual contributions we'd need two new specs:

- `disable_littles_and_missing_rate` — keeps cross_column only.
- `disable_littles_and_cross_column` — keeps missing_rate only.

That's the obvious follow-up if the dissertation needs the precise
per-group decomposition. **Not run here** — the headline question is
answered (pipeline contributes), and the per-group decomposition is
secondary to the main story.

## Decision-rule application

Pre-registered (PLANNED.md §2 at launch):

> If the 95 % CI on `all_disabled − baseline` EXCLUDES zero in the
> negative direction (baseline wins clearly): the pipeline contributes.
> Report the feature-group decomposition to identify which groups
> carry the lift.

That branch fires. CI [−0.095, −0.021] excludes zero; baseline (= the
surviving `disable_littles`) wins clearly. Pipeline contributes.

The decomposition story is more nuanced than the rule anticipated:
the lift comes from the non-MCAR groups, with the MCAR slot
(specifically MLE) actively harming. ADR 0004 (drafted alongside this
writeup) codifies the implications.
