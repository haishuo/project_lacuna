## Goal

Confirm or reject the 2026-04-19 morning finding that `disable_littles`
beats `baseline (MLE)` on held-out accuracy, ECE, and generalization
gap. At n=5 the accuracy-delta 95% CI was [−0.030, +0.120] — narrowly
includes zero. ECE and gap CIs both excluded zero. At n=10, Wilcoxon
has enough combinatorial headroom to reach p<0.05 when a real effect
exists (minimum two-sided p at n=10 is ~0.002), turning the result from
"strong suggestion" into "defensible at conventional significance."

## Hypothesis

At n=10, the paired accuracy CI for `disable_littles` − `baseline_MLE`
excludes zero in the positive direction; the sign-consistent pattern
from the 4-of-5-seeds result at n=5 holds at n=10.

## Interpretation

- **Accuracy.** CI narrowed from n=5 [−0.030, +0.120] to n=10
  [−0.008, +0.104] but still includes zero. Point estimate and
  direction are preserved (8/10 seeds favor disable, vs. 4/5 at n=5)
  and effect size is medium (d_z ≈ +0.55), but we do not reach
  conventional significance for accuracy. This is the "inconclusive on
  accuracy" branch forecast in HANDOFF-2026-04-20.md.
- **ECE.** CI excludes zero ([−0.105, −0.008]). `disable_littles` is
  clearly better calibrated. This is the most decisive of the three
  metrics.
- **Generalization gap.** At n=5 this CI excluded zero; at n=10 it
  straddles zero by a hair ([−0.093, +0.008]). The point estimate and
  direction are stable; the earlier n=5 exclusion was slightly
  optimistic given the true variance.
- **Overall.** Sign and magnitude of the n=5 finding are preserved at
  n=10 for all three metrics. The n=5 morning claim that
  `disable_littles` wins is not refuted, but the accuracy and gap
  effects are not defensible at conventional α=0.05 on n=10 paired
  seeds. ECE is.

## Decision taken

Per the pre-registered decision rule, accuracy CI still includes zero,
so we do not immediately write an ADR superseding ADR 0002. But the
direction has held under more power, and ECE is a clean win. The
question "does the Little's slot help" is still open, consistent with
the handoff's "likely inconclusive" branch.

**Next step (per HANDOFF-2026-04-20.md §"Action depending on n10-followup outcome"):**
run the `mcar-alternatives-bakeoff` Stage 1 screening — if any
alternative MCAR-test family beats `disable_littles`, we have a
productive replacement; if nothing does, we have a confident
"no MCAR-test feature family is useful in this architecture" result,
which is itself an ADR-worthy finding. Pending user confirmation.
