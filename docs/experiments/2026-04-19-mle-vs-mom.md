# 2026-04-19 — Real Little's (MLE vs MoM) vs no-Little's, 5 seeds

- **Status:** Complete
- **Date run:** 2026-04-19 (~5.3 h wall-clock; 21.3 min/run mean)
- **Commit state:** post-ADR-0002 (cached real Little's) and ADR-0003
  (dual-method MLE + MoM cache); feature extractor has 3 groups / 9 scalars
  after ADR 0001 removed pointbiserial and distributional.

## Goal

Two questions, answered in one sweep:

1. **Primary:** Does the method-of-moments MCAR test (`mom_mcar_test`,
   ~30–50× faster than MLE) give comparable downstream classification
   accuracy to MLE (`little_mcar_test`)? If yes, switch the default to MoM.
2. **Secondary:** Has upgrading the Little's-slot from heuristic to real
   Little's preserved the +6% contribution measured in the 2026-04-17
   ablation? (Sanity check on ADR 0002.)

## Interpretation

**Headline finding: the Little's-slot feature — in either MLE or MoM form —
is actively harmful in the current Lacuna architecture.**

Three pieces of evidence pointing the same direction for the
`disable_littles` vs `baseline (MLE)` contrast:

1. **Accuracy:** +6.1% paired delta (d_z=0.61, large effect). CI [−0.030,
   +0.120] narrowly includes zero.
2. **ECE:** −5.7% (better calibrated). 95% CI [−0.110, −0.000] excludes
   zero at the boundary. d_z=−0.81 (large effect).
3. **Generalization gap:** −5.1% (memorizes less). CI [−0.104, −0.003]
   excludes zero. d_z=−0.78.

These three effects are mechanistically coherent: if the cached Little's
scalars were letting the model fingerprint (dataset, generator) pairs
to memorize labels, removing them should (a) close the train–val gap,
(b) reduce overconfident predictions (ECE), and (c) force reliance on
generalisable features (val accuracy). All three directions match.

**Reverses the 2026-04-17 conclusion.** That experiment, using the
*heuristic* (median-split SMD) at the Little's slot, found
`disable_littles` *hurt* by −6.4%. This experiment, using the *real*
Little's MCAR test at the same slot, finds `disable_littles` *helps* by
+6.1%. A 12-point swing between feature implementations for the same
architectural slot: the heuristic was a genuinely useful ML feature; the
statistically rigorous test is not.

**Two plausible explanations (not mutually exclusive):**

- **(a) Sentinel contamination.** 16.4% of MLE cache entries are
  sentinels `(stat=0, p=1)`. The sentinel-set is non-random — it clusters
  on high-d datasets where EM struggles. A model can learn to use
  "got a sentinel" as a dataset fingerprint, driving memorization. MoM's
  0.9% sentinel rate would reduce but not eliminate this; the +2.2%
  accuracy gap between MoM and MLE is consistent with partial
  sentinel-driven memorization in the MLE baseline.

- **(b) Real Little's has ML-unfriendly properties.** Little's
  chi-squared is unbounded, scales with missingness-pattern count, and is
  designed for hypothesis testing — not feature engineering. The
  heuristic was bounded and smooth; a classifier may prefer that.

### MLE vs. MoM specifically

Largely moot given the bigger finding. MoM numerically beats MLE on all
three metrics (accuracy +2.2%, ECE −3.1%, gap −2.5%) but no CI excludes
zero. If removing the Little's-slot entirely beats both, the
MLE-vs-MoM contest doesn't affect Lacuna's default. Recorded here
because the sweep measured it; not load-bearing for dissertation.

## Decisions taken

- **None yet** — the finding is strong enough in effect size to motivate
  action but the accuracy-delta CI touches zero at n=5. Queueing a
  higher-power re-sweep before writing an ADR that would remove or
  refactor the Little's-slot. See `PLANNED.md` experiment `n10-followup`.
